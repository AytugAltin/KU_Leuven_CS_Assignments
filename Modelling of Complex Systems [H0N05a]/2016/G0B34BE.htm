Knowledge and the Web (B-KUL-G0B34B)

6 ECTSEnglish43 First termCannot be taken as part of an examination contract
Berendt Bettina
POC Computerwetenschappen

  • Previous knowledge

  • Order of Enrolment

  • Is included in these courses of study

Aims

Students will understand and master relevant concepts and techniques of current modelling, interconnecting and processing large volumes of data on the Web. They will understand the potentials, limitations, and risks inherent in assembling, combining, and processing such heterogeneous data in globally interconnected environments. They will have gone through the process of collecting and analysing data based on a knowlegde question, in a hands-on way and with real-world data.

Previous knowledge

Knowledge as acquired in a Computerwetenschappen or Informatica Bachelor at KU Leuven or an equivalent programme. In particular:

  • Conceptual modelling (EER, UML) and translation to a logical model
  • Basics of logic and probability theory
  • Programming
  • SQL
  • Working with command-line interfaces, menu-driven interfaces and GUIs

Students who are unsure about their knowledge in these areas are prepared to self-study them before or at the beginning of the course. When in doubt, they contact the course director.
In addition, students have an interest in the Web and real-world data and in thinking critically about the potentials and limitations of data science. Students are reliable group members who are able to engage in, and enjoy, joint work as much as individual work.

Content

What does it mean to describe the World Wide Web as "the largest knowledge base that has ever existed"? How can computers use and process the data on the Web for enhancing knowledge, and what challenges are found along the way? This course aims at familiarising students with state-of-the-art theoretical and practical methods for addressing these questions. The course starts from data on the Web that are suited as sources of knowledge, with a special focus on ontological modelling and the framework of the Semantic Web/Linked Open Data. It discusses
the importance and key challenges of making data interoperable and of then processing such combined data in a way that goes beyond retrieval towards the inference of new knowledge. It also discusses some effects and side-effects of this large-scale data/knowledge processing.

The main subtopics are

Introduction

  • Data, information and knowledge
  • Web data and applications
  • Conceptual modelling

Data interoperability and semantics

  • The Semantic Web
  • Linked Open Data
  • Ontologies and ontology modelling using Semantic-Web languages

Data heterogeneity and combining data

  • Schema/ontology matching
  • Issues of data quality

From data to knowledge

  • Deductive and inductive inferences
  • Inductive inferences, especially using knowledge discovery from data(bases) and Web Mining
  • Potentials and limitations of "engineering knowledge from data"

The lectures respectively exercises will focus on the theoretical/overarching respectively practical aspects of these subtopics. Details will be worked out in accordance with the participants’ prior knowledge and interests, and with the datasets used.

These topics will be extended both for the 6-studypoints version and for the 4-studypoints version of the course. The extension for the 6-studypoints version is described in the "Lectures 2 / Exercises 2" unit of this course; it consists of additions to the topics above and a "Data in context" part. Participants of the 4-studypoints version will also take part in the "Data in context" part, but have this counted towards the "Privacy and Big Data" course. Details can be found at the instructor's Web page and will be discussed in the course.

 

Course material

Study materials will comprise:

  • a detailed slide set
  • articles and literature
  • software and data

Materials will be made available online (to the extent possible).

Content

In the exercises, you will deal with practical versions of the contents of the lecture. You will work with different data, languages, and tools. Tasks will proceed from relatively closed-question exercises to more open-ended activities shaped by your interests and questions. You will model a domain, collect and integrate data, and analyse it.

Course material

Slides, research papers and other documents; datasets. Everything is available online or made available.

Content

In this part of the lecture, content from the basic lecture will be deepened and broadened by current topics and by considerations on data in context. Specifically, additional contents will be covered on Data heterogeneity and combining data, such as dealing with inconsistencies, and on Inferences, such as inferences on the Semantic Web.

The "Data in context" part will discuss the following issues:

  • Data publishing (especially on the Web) and data mining and privacy
  • Data publishing and mining and discrimination

  • Explanation

Explanation

The students will work through a series of homeworks to make up their own small project, which develops a knowledge question along with a series of modelling and processing steps that aim at deriving this knowledge from Web data. The answers will comprise modelling, programming, analyses, and reporting (written and in presentations), and students’ progress on the homeworks and in the lectures and exercise sessions will be the topic of ongoing evaluation (permanente evaluatie, schriftelijk en mondeling). Students work in groups and individually.
